Chapter 1

1. What is Design?

  • Definitions:
    • A creative planning process leading to useful products/systems (with criteria and constraints).
    • Involves decision-making under uncertainty and incomplete information.
    • Produces unexpected solutions using imagination and modeling.
  • Perspectives:
    • Noun: A plan, blueprint, or outcome.
    • Verb: The act of creating the plan.

8. Fundamental Concepts

  • Abstraction:
    • Procedural (how tasks are done)
    • Data (object representation)
  • Architecture: Overall software structure (e.g., modules)
  • Patterns: Reusable solutions (e.g., Prototype pattern)
  • Modularity: Splitting code into independent modules
  • Information Hiding: Limit module communication to essentials
  • Functional Independence:
    • High cohesion (single-purpose modules)
    • Low coupling (minimal inter-module dependencies)
